The Challenge

Extensive clearing of forests and woodlands for agriculture have degenerated our ecosystem. We know this story well. We're from the Wheatbelt of Western Australia.

Ecosystem Degradation

The removal of trees has resulted in widespread salinity erosion, soil is being leached of nutrients and conventional chemical farming practices have destroyed soil biology.

Limited Opporunities 

Food is less nutritious and farming communities are shrinking as jobs disappear ...

Biodiversity Under Threat

Extensive clearing, coupled with convensional farming practices that degrade the ecosystem has resulted in unprecedented loss of biodiversity and lead to extinction.

The Solution

Restoring ecosystem health through regenerative farming and biodiversity conservation. Tackling this challenge requires a management re-think of both farmland and nature in tandem.
The solution can be framed within Commonland’s 3 zone approach, which we have adapted for Australian conditions: 

Nature Zone

Protect, enhance and grow biodiversity conservation areas within farmland, including threatened species habitat.

Resilient Regenerative Zone

Permanent agricultural systems that maximise biodiversity and carbon drawdown, such as silvopasture (livestock and trees), polyculture forestry or perennial pasture.

Intensive Regenerative Zone

The transition from conventional to regenerative agricultural farming practises that improves soil health, sequesters carbon and promotes above and below ground diversity.

Project Spotlight

Andover is a 1787 hectare broadacre cropping and sheep farm near Kojonup in Western Australia’s Wheatbelt.
Falling within the south western australian biodiversity hotspot, one of only 36 terrestrial ecosystems worldwide deemed biologically rich yet threatened. 

Regenerate Andover with WOA Biodiversity Units

By purchasing WOA Biodiversity Units (WBU’s) you enable a farmer to protect, enhance and grow the biodiverse bushland area within the nature zone of a farm. 

This unique habitat area forms an integral part of the wider ecosystem. You are also supporting regenerative farmers on their land restoration journey, as they transition farming practises to improve the health and resilience of the land.

Each WBU represents a 1.5m square area of land of significant environmental value that has been placed under a conservation covenant and agreed management plan. The covenant and management plan secure the long term preservation of the site and ensure the biodiversity value is protected in perpetuity. Vegetation types may include forests, grasslands, mallees, saltmarshes, scrubs, shrublands, wetlands, and woodlands. WBU’s are an innovative and unique way for organisations to preserve a tangible section of Australia’s unique natural habitat.

Commonland's 4 Returns Holistic Framework

4 Returns is a science-based framework that is proven in practice. Developed in close collaboration with leading scientific institutes, business schools, farmers and experts, 4 Returns transforms degraded ecosystems by focusing on 4 key returns over the course of a single generation, or 20 years. Identifies 3 distinct landscape zones – natural, economic and combined.

Regen Network's Biodiversity Credit Platorm

Credits issued on Regen Network's biodiversity credit registry help to align economic incentives with ecological health by lowering the cost of credit issuace through remote sensing based verification of ecological outcomes, blockchain based technology for robust tracability and auditability. Savings are passed on to stewards, help accelerate project listing, credit issuance, and verification.
