Andover is a 1787 hectare broadacre cropping and sheep farm near Kojonup in Western Australia’s Wheatbelt.
Falling within the south western australian biodiversity hotspot, one of only 36 terrestrial ecosystems worldwide deemed biologically rich yet threatened. 

Regenerate Andovar with Australian Biodiversity Units

By purchasing Ausralian Biodiversity Units for project Andovar. Organizations can preserve a tangible section of Australia's unique habitat, support farming communities through jobs, and replenish depleated soils.

Each ABU represents a 1.5m square area of land of significant environmental value that has been placed under a conservation covenant and agreed management plan. The covenant and management plan secure the long term preservation of the site and ensure the biodiversity value is protected in perpetuity. Vegetation types may include forests, grasslands, mallees, saltmarshes, scrubs, shrublands, wetlands, and woodlands. ABU’s are an innovative and unique way for organisations to preserve a tangible section of Australia’s unique natural habitat.

Conservation Covenant protects land for 100 years minimum.

Land is protected and managed to mitigate fire risk.

Threatened species sanctuary specific to each area.

Commonland's 4 Returns Holistic Framework

4 Returns is a science-based framework that is proven in practice. Developed in close collaboration with leading scientific institutes, business schools, farmers and experts, 4 Returns transforms degraded ecosystems by focusing on 4 key returns over the course of a single generation, or 20 years. Identifies 3 distinct landscape zones – natural, economic and combined.

About

Government Endorsed Methodology

The Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999 (the EPBC Act) is the Australian Government’s central piece of environmental legislation. It provides a legal framework to protect and manage nationally and internationally important flora, fauna, ecological communities and heritage places — defined in the EPBC Act as matters of national environmental significance.

Regen Network's Biodiversity Credit Platorm

Credits issued on Regen Network's biodiversity credit registry help to align economic incentives with ecological health by lowering the cost of credit issuace through remote sensing based verification of ecological outcomes, blockchain based technology for robust tracability and auditability. Savings are passed on to stewards, help accelerate project listing, credit issuance, and verification.
